Born : February 19, 1630 (Shivneri)
Died : April 3, 1680 (Raigad)


Indian ruler and a member of the Bhonsle dynasty.


Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, born on February 19, 1630, at Shivneri Fort, was the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India. From a young age, Shivaji aspired to establish an independent Maratha kingdom, embarking on his mission by capturing the Torna Fort in 1645. Renowned for his innovative military tactics, including guerrilla warfare, Shivaji successfully challenged the larger and better-equipped armies of the Mughal Empire and the Deccan Sultanates. His daring escape from Agra after being held captive by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in 1666 is legendary. Shivaji's coronation as Chhatrapati in 1674 at Raigad Fort marked the formal establishment of Maratha rule, underpinned by an efficient administrative system and a formidable naval force to safeguard his kingdom.

Shivaji Maharaj's legacy extends beyond his military and political achievements. He is celebrated for his progressive governance, emphasis on welfare and justice, and the promotion of Marathi and Sanskrit cultures. His life and deeds have been immortalized in Indian folklore, literature, and history, with numerous statues, forts, and public places named in his honor. Shivaji's forts, such as Raigad, Sindhudurg, and Pratapgad, remain popular tourist attractions, symbolizing his enduring influence. Passing away on April 3, 1680, Shivaji left behind a resilient Maratha Empire that continued to flourish under his successors, inspiring contemporary political and social movements in India.


Biography of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj


  • Birth: Born on February 19, 1630, at Shivneri Fort in Maharashtra, India.
  • Parents: Son of Shahaji Bhosale, a Maratha general, and Jijabai, who played a crucial role in his upbringing.
  • Early Conquests: Captured his first fort, Torna, at the age of 15, initiating his quest for an independent Maratha kingdom.
  • Military Tactics: Known for his innovative guerrilla warfare strategies, which allowed him to outmaneuver larger armies.
  • Coronation: Crowned as Chhatrapati of the Maratha Kingdom on June 6, 1674, at Raigad Fort, establishing a formal rule.
  • Administration: Implemented a progressive and efficient administrative system, including the Ashta Pradhan (Council of Eight Ministers).
  • Naval Strength: Established a strong navy to protect his kingdom from foreign invasions, particularly by the Portuguese and the British.
  • Mughal Conflicts: Engaged in significant conflicts with the Mughal Empire, notably with Emperor Aurangzeb; his escape from captivity in Agra in 1666 is legendary.
  • Cultural Impact: Promoted Marathi and Sanskrit cultures, and his legacy is celebrated in Indian folklore, literature, and history.
  • Death: Passed away on April 3, 1680, leaving behind a resilient Maratha Empire that continued to grow under his successors.
